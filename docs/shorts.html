Trying to focus on art to understand the human experience better.

Long Live the Republic - 7/10 - Metrograph 6/6 - Benny Katz gave me 2 free tickets to movis recently, so I am kinda getting into the artsy cinema. The first one was Long Live the Republic, which was a Czech movie from maybe 1950 and it is about something with the Soviet Union and Nazis, but I'm not sure really. Maybe it was about the transition from the rule of the Nazis to the Soviet Union, and everybody was happy about it except for the kid because he was friends with the Nazis. I don't know, I was lost on a lot of things. The kid was being beat by hid dad, and he was bullied, but he was clever so he was doing these cute things to get out of trouble. There isn't really a plot line. He was running away from home a lot of the movie. Then he had to hide his horse from whoever the bad guy was, and somebody stole his horse. Then, he had to get it back cuz his Dad would beat him up if he lost the horse. The confusing plot dragged out forever. Then, he befriended a Russian who took his horse. He was nice and fed the kid, then they rode on a motorcyle they found together. Then the Russian is shot and the rest of the movie is war. I think there were explosions and cuts to flashbacks, but it was hard for me to follow. Still emotional nonetheless. I remember thinking it was cool, but I didn't really understand or feel affected by the emotional context. I guess it was showing how sad his life is, but maybe there was a bright light that he will survive. In this review it doesnt sound like I liked it at all. But that is what I do with movies. If I went, it's automatically 5/10.

Atlantic City - 8/10 - Metrograph 6/14 - I just got back from this one, but I am high so let's see how much I can remember. It is listed as a romance online, but the romance is not the plotline. It's pretty complicated, but the characters are the main girl who is hot and really excited to work at a casino, her ex husband who is having a kid with the hot girl's sister and they have cult 1960s vibes, there are a bunch of mobsters, and an old guy who used to be a criminal and is now poor and caring for a crazy older lady. So, old guy is in love with the hot girl, and he also sells the drugs that the ex husband had, then they get involved with the mob because the drugs were stolen. The old guy ends up being the bad character because he gets rich and tried to win over the hot girl and it has such a weird energy. Then he kills two mobsters and gets way too excited about it, so the hot girl leaves and he ends up with the old lady. I don't know if the acting or production or something was bad because it felt so cheap. But, I was still entertained the whole time, which is shocking. In conclusion, I liked the mob in this movie, I hated how much of a creep the old guy was and how helpless they made the female lead. I didn't feel any strong emotions about this or have any deeper insights into anything, but I still loved it. Also, when I walked out of a movie theatre I feel so cool. I see everything in the context of the movie and I stare dramatically like I'm being recorded. I would keep going to the metrograph just to experience the five minutes after where you feel like you're in a different world. Afterwards, it was raining and I walked in the rain with music and got an ice cream cone. It felt unreal.
