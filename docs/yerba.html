Yerba Madre Camp

I'm trying to take away something from each trip, so I'm not just aimlessly floating around with nothing to show for it. So, I had my free adult summer camp in the Poconos with Yerba Madre last week. I went alone and there were like 250 people there, and most of them didn't know each other. It was actually one of the best experiences ever. We all met at the Newark airport, then took a shuttle to the camp. As soon as I got there, people were sitting together and introducing themselves. The first people I met were Imoya from Houston, Michael from Ohio, and Jane from somewhere in South America. I actually can't believe how nice everybody was. There were some people who were really outdoorsy and fit the vibe that I thought was gonna be there, but there were also just a lot of normal people who liked doing crazy things. Like there was no information about this camp given to us, it was entirely free, and nobody knew each other. Everybody thought it was going to be a cult. The camp grounds were unreal. Everything surrounds a lake. They dropped us off at a barn and gave us a bag of merch and a light up bracelet that said our names. They had a lawn with mocktails made with yerba mate, and a charcuterie spread that was 20 feet long. My cabin was all the way on the other side of the lake, next to the pool, which was a 5-10 minute walk. I think I got the best cabin. I chose a bed that looked at the lake, and at night I went stargazing on the doc. The stars were unbelievable- we were in the middle of nowhere. The first day felt extra culty. There was an opening ceremony, then dinner was so good, then there was a camp fire. You could start to figure out how this was transactional- it was basically a content farm- we were recorded everywhere we went for promos. I met new people at every single event. It was one of those places where you get desensitized to interaction, so I would walk up to people to introduce myself all the time. On day 2 or 3 I had to stop cuz I didn't know who I had talked to yet, and I didn't want to accidentally reintroduce myself. So at the campfire they assigned us to different "houses" for something called the cebador games. I was in house Sol, which was headed by a guy named Jack, who is the main marketer for Yerba. I found out he is a 32 year old gay man. Mostly everybody there was mid twenties, but there were a few in their 30s, I met a 19 year old, and a 50 year old lady. The camp was majority women which was so great. After we got our houses we went for a night hike, which was so silly. We were hiking with no lights in a group of 200 people. Around this time we noticed we had unlimited Yerba and no water. So I got there at 6, I think I drank 3 yerbs, which have much more caffeine than I thought, and I could not find water. So we did the campfire and everything, and nobody could sleep at night.

We had 2 full days of activities. There was obviously the pool and lake, they had soccer and vollyball, hiking, mountain biking, arts and crafts, and kayaking to do in our free time. Then we had the games where we competed in challenges. The games were super silly I guess we just had them for structure and comraderie. I tried to do as many things as possible. It was only 2 days so I thought I could just not rest, but eventually I was exhausted. I was so bad at mountainbiking but it was so fun. We played so many games. And we had unlimited food. I don't know what else to say it was great. Oh yes reflections. 1. Everybody was flirting obviously. I could not tell who was flirting and there were 2 masc girls that I was trying to flirt with and I think they were flirting back and I made it very awkward. One was Shea who kinda worked for yerba. She was super sweet, she started all the convos with me. She lives in LA and does pottery and odd jobs. I told her that I hated LA and I also think I talked too much, so not great. Then the other one was a nonbinary lesbian named Ray. I didn't really talk to them, but I did try. We made dyke bracelets together and then they asked to hang out at dyke march but I said I would be out of town. So looked promising, but I didn't get any good opportunities. I wonder if I am asexual. 2. People have crazy lives. I can't believe how much of a bubble I'm in in real life. Like a weird life path to me is doing art school instead of engineering school. But being at hostels and at camp has exposed me to people doing crazy shit. When I asked Shea what she does she just responded "well now I'm at camp". Like people lived nomadic lifestyles. People worked in industries that I didn't know existed. One girl was in the coffee buisiness whatever that means. It's so reassuring. I thought I could be good with structure, like getting a job asap because I didn't think there was an alternative. Like I know people live crazy lives but I never thought it would be me. I didn't realize you could be smart and educated and still all over the place. There were some girls that had just graduated college. I watched another girl sign a contract for a new job. I watched another girl quit her job. My other friend just got fired. Everybody was in such a weird interim space, but not worried at all. I feel like I have no plan and nothing going on at all, but everybody was so shocked that I finished engineering school, nobody questioned what I was doing at all. I feel less anxious about my future, even though it remains a huge issue. 3. This one is actually bad. Mom texted Jack and I on the third day saying she has breast cancer. It's stage 0, so it's a lump, but it hasn't spread and it's 99% curable. I don't know if I reacted correctly because cancer in movies feels like "will they survive?". But this is so treatable and it's not chemo, so it's just a matter of logistics. I'm spending the next month in Taipei, so I was trying to figure out if I should cancel the trip to take care of mom. But, the consultation with the doctor isn't until July 7th, and we don't know how long after the surgery is going to be. I'm not worried, but I am the most worried of anybody. Jack for some reason didn't talk to anybody for the last week, and he never responded to mom about this. I was so fucking mad. I asked mom if she wanted me to come home this weekend just cuz, and she said "if you want to, I'm mostly worried about your brother". So that's just so cool. This must have been an issue for the rest of my life, but I don't remember it being this bad. Also, if Jack doesn't do anything then both of us are going to be blamed for wanting our mother to die or something. Ok, so that news happened during camp, but it doesn't concern Yerba. Basically, camp was the best thing ever. And going into this I didn't feel confident in my social skills because I spend most of my time alone, so I struggle to interact with people. But I think I did a good job, and I left feeling very confident about myself, which is new. Also, I made 2 friends that I went out with in NYC the night we got back. They made a comment that I was so bubbly, which used to be the way people described me all the time. I thought I had lost it for some reason and I could never bring myself to be that outgoing and happy again and it was so nice to hear. I didn't feel like I was forcing it at all, I just felt happy.
