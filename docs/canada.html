Impulsive Trip to Canada!

Two weeks ago maybe Ella was in Costa Rica and my family had left and I had nothing to do and literally nobody was here. I freaked out and bought a bus ticket to Montreal. Montreal was in the Stanley Cup or something so I thought that would be fun, but I didn't watch it at all really. So basically, I found out I could take an $80 overnight bus to Montreal, an $80 overnight from Montreal to Toronto, then an $80 overnight back to NYC, stopping at Niagara Falls. So this was my plan, but I had no timeline, I only bought the ticket to Montreal. I also dyed my hair pink an hour before I left, and I forget how much people stare at you with pink hair. But also, when I travel to another country, I'm very aware of how other people are perceiving me so it was a concerning combination of decisions.

My bus arrived at 8 am, and I couldn't check in until 3pm, so I just wandered around. I first found a coffee shop, where I planned my walk for the day. I knew they spoke French, but I didn't know how French the culture actually was. It was a weird combination because I couldn't find a good coffee shop originally. It looked like people were at work and I had to go to a chain. But after this, I went to the National Archives- tbh I might have been in the library, I don't know I was very lost- where the range of topics just seemed so French to me. A lot of Philosophy, all the sciences, and really not a lot of like modern fantasy stuff. There were bookstores all over the city that all had great selections. So, my walk took me through a college, where I stopped at their cathedral, the library, and an art building. I did a 45 minute walk to notre Dame, which took me through China Town and old Montreal. I talked to grandma on the phone and she told me about when she visited Montreal for the 1967 World Fair. They built a giant dome on an island which I got to see. The church was gorgeous. I don't know if I can embed videos, but there was an organ playing, it was surreal. I don't know if I need to recap the whole trip in this much detail, but the first impression feels important. My hostel was in a neighborhood called little Portugal. So, I got a Bahn Mi and walked probably another hour. ABout 10 minutes from my hostel there is a huge park. I don't remember what it is called. I think they had an olympic stadium in the middle. It was a nice place to walk but I did not see any landmarks. My neighborhood felt especially European compared to the rest of the city. There were long spiral staircases, and cute places to sit outside. Then I got to my hostel,which might have been my favorite part of the trip. It was so cute. Everything was made of wood, so it looked like grandma and grandpas old house.
